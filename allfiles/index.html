1. The A.R.T. Principle

Every-time you're presented with a premise, it's your opportunity to Accept it, Reject it, or Tweak it.

2. The Truth Principle

Truth is that which holds no contradiction, therefore it's worthy of your dedication to it.

3. The Destiny Principle

Your decisions determine your destiny.

4. The Cutting Principle

The essence of a decision is always cutting... separating one option away from another.

5. The ONLY Thing Principle

The ONLY thing in life you have to do consciously is, make decisions, cutting one option away from one another.

6. The Inability Principle

The ONLY thing that keeps you from ALL you're meant to be, do, and have is an INABILITY to make better decisions faster.

7. The One Reason Principle

The ONLY 3 Reasons Anyone Ever Communicates: 1. To Share Pain 2. To Share Pleasure 3. To Make Decisions.

8. The Break The Chain Principle

Want progress? Build it. Break it. Make it Better!

9. The Quality Question Principle

The quality of your life is contingent upon the quality of questions you ask yourself and others and the corresponding quality of answers you get in return.

10. The Progress Principle

Success in ANY endeavor is guaranteed IF you can identify and amend your critical erroneous assumptions... early enough.

11. The Contrast Principle

The Brain Needs Contrast To Make A Decision.

12. The Scalability Principle

Develop decision-making criteria within systems for all repetitive decisions.

13. The Imagination Principle

The realm of imagination is the origin of ALL creation.

14. The Heuristic Principle

IF you need to speed up a decision-making process and you can afford a margin of error.

THEN employ a Heuristic [a mental short cut].

"Examples that employ heuristics include using trial and error, a rule of thumb, an educated guess, an intuitive judgment, a guesstimate, profiling, or common sense." (Wikipedia.org)

15. The Discernment Principle

Exercise your spiritual senses to discern good from evil while... staying focused on that which is good.

16. The Critical Thinker's Principle

IF you establish, secure, and exercise your freedom to question any of the variables under scrutiny in the argument at hand,

THEN you'll drastically increase your probabilities of coming to trustworthy conclusions.

17. The Fallacy Finder Principle

IF you want to build upon truth,

THEN you ought to welcome a search for and identification of fallacious reasonings in your arguments...

SO that you develop a team that builds upon facts rather than assumptions.

18. The Cognitive Bias Buster Principle

The more familiar you are with cognitive biases the easier it will be to identify and not be unduly influenced by them.

19. The Wisdom Principle

IF Wisdom is the ability to make distinctions and predictions that lead to righteous results and,

IF "with much wisdom comes much sorrow" and,

IF you want righteous results more than anything else!

THEN You'll be willing to go through the sorrow aspect of wisdom to get the righteous results.

20. The Minimalism Principle

IF you develop minimalistic habits,

THEN you'll reduce decision making fatigue.

21. The Resourcefulness Principle

Your constraint is never due to a lack of resources. It's always due to a lack of resourcefulness.

22. The Excuse Yourself Principle

When you have a high level of emotional enmeshment or a conflict of intrest it can at times serve everyone best to excuse yourself from the decision(the surgery, the judgment, the lawyering, etx.)to allow another to make the decision instead.

23. The Biological Breakthrough Principle

The better your body is functioning the more attention you have to allocate towards breaking through to your next game-changing conclusion.

24. The Emotional Shift Principle

    IF you feel a negative emotion
    THEN it's a sign to change what you're:
    Thinking
  • perception
  • self talk
  • assessments
  • focus
    Doing
  • words
  • gestures
  • tones
  • activities.

25. The Identity Transformation Principle

Your identity determines the nature of your choices.

26. The Scarcity Principle

Scarcity forces decisions.

27. The Be, Do, Have Principle

    Everything You're Meant to Be Do and Have Can Be Found:
  • In The Context of Relationships,
  • On The Other Side of a Series of Decision, and...
  • Just Outside of Your Comfort Zone.
